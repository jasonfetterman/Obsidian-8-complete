1. PURPOSE

Define the Energy-Aware Navigation Model (EANM) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures missions are executed within battery and thermal constraints
Integrates actuator load, terrain, and energy model data
Coordinates with Mission Compute, Reactive Controller, and BMS
Provides deterministic energy budgeting and predictive load management
2. INPUT PARAMETERS
Parameter	Source	Notes
Battery SOC / Voltage	Smart BMS	Real-time update
Battery Type	Deployment profile	LiFePO4 / LiPo curves
Actuator Power Demand	Servo Bank Architecture / ROS2	Per-joint torque
Terrain Resistance	Sensor suite / terrain maps	Friction and slope
Thermal Status	Thermal Management Model	Adjust allowed motion
Mission Objective	Mission Compute	Target path, duration

4. NAVIGATION ADJUSTMENTS
Speed Scaling: slower motion for low SOC or high temperature
Gait Selection: tripod / neutral / stair modes based on terrain and energy cost
Segment Prioritization: favor lower-energy paths if mission constraints allow
Dynamic Replanning: reactive adjustment in case of unexpected load spikes
5. INTEGRATION WITH CONTROL AND SAFETY
Mission Compute: executes energy-aware trajectories
Reactive Controller: overrides motion if thermal or actuator limits exceeded
BMS: provides real-time SOC, voltage, and temperature constraints
Thermal Management Model: informs allowable load and duty cycles
Energy-aware decisions never override hardware kill-line or reactive safety stops
6. FAILURE MODES AND MITIGATION
Failure Mode	Cause	Mitigation
Energy underestimation	Incorrect SOC / terrain model	Conservative margin + recalculation
Unexpected actuator load	Terrain irregularity	Adaptive gait / speed adjustment
Thermal limit exceeded	Sustained high torque	Reactive Controller stop + speed reduction
FMEA references: FM-003 (battery undervoltage), FM-004 (servo overheating)
7. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Simulate full mission paths under varying SOC and terrain conditions
Monitor predicted vs actual energy consumption
Verify speed and gait adjustments trigger appropriately
Test fail-safe response when energy drops below threshold
Log and audit all energy-aware navigation decisions
8. DESIGN INTENT
Enable safe, predictable mission completion within energy constraints
Integrate fully with BMS, actuators, thermal models, and reactive safety
Minimize risk of mission abort due to energy depletion or thermal overload
Provide scalable framework for multi-terrain and multi-mission deployment
Maintain deterministic behavior and audit-ready operation
