1. PURPOSE

Define the Spare Parts Strategy (SPS) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensure rapid replacement of failed components to maintain operational readiness
Support fleet management, lifecycle sustainability, and cost control
Integrate with maintenance, reliability, and configuration management
2. OBJECTIVES
Maintain adequate inventory levels of critical spare parts
Enable rapid field replacement and minimize downtime
Track spare parts usage and link to serial-numbered units
Ensure spare parts comply with baseline configurations
Forecast replacement needs based on MTBF, mission profiles, and usage patterns
3. SPARE PARTS CATEGORIZATION

3.1 Critical Components:

Actuators, servo banks, and drive motors
Battery packs (LiFePO4 / LiPo)
Frame modules and structural subassemblies
Sensor suite (IMU, cameras, LiDAR)
Control computer boards and communication modules

3.2 Standard Components:

Wiring harnesses and connectors
Mounting brackets, fasteners, and protective covers
Thermal management parts (fans, heatsinks, coolant lines)
Software flash keys or deployment media

3.3 Consumables:

Lubricants, cleaning supplies, and sealants
Backup cables and fuse elements
4. INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
Maintain a minimum stock level for all critical components (typically ≥2 units per deployed unit)
Track inventory via serial numbers, batch IDs, and version
Implement first-in, first-out (FIFO) for parts usage
Update configuration and maintenance records when parts are installed or replaced
5. REPLACEMENT PROCEDURES

5.1 Field Replacement:

Authorized technicians follow documented procedure for removing and installing parts
Verify part version matches baseline for unit’s deployment profile
Log all replacements, including date, technician, and serial number

5.2 Depot-Level Replacement:

Complex components or assemblies sent to central depot for repair or calibration
Replace failed parts with verified units from inventory
Update all configuration records and maintenance logs

5.3 Calibration & Verification:

Post-replacement calibration of sensors, actuators, and energy systems
Verify functional operation against acceptance criteria
Ensure system logs updated for audit
6. FORECASTING AND LIFECYCLE INTEGRATION
Use operational logs and FMEA data to predict part replacement cycles
Align spare parts procurement with Phase 2 Industrialization and Phase 3 Deployment
Review usage trends annually and adjust stock levels
Ensure spares support fleet readiness and mission continuity
7. DESIGN INTENT
Minimize downtime and maximize operational availability
Ensure parts compatibility and adherence to baseline configurations
Maintain traceable records for inventory and replacement actions
Support long-term fleet sustainability and reliability
