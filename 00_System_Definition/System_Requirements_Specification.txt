OBSIDIAN-8 V3
System_Requirements_Specification.txt
Revision: V3.3
Status: Authoritative

------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define formal, testable system‑level requirements for OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.  
All design, code, and validation activities must trace to these requirements.

Each requirement is uniquely identified and version‑controlled.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. REQUIREMENT IDENTIFICATION FORMAT
------------------------------------------------------------

Format: REQ‑[CATEGORY]‑[NUMBER]

Categories:  
MECH – Mechanical ELEC – Electrical CTRL – Control SAFE – Safety  
NAV – Navigation ENER – Energy SW – Software COMM – Communication  
OPS – Operational LIFE – Lifecycle  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. MECHANICAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑MECH‑001  System shall maintain structural integrity under defined deployment load.  
REQ‑MECH‑002  System shall withstand torsional loading within stability envelope.  
REQ‑MECH‑003  Center of gravity shall remain within defined stability polygon during stance.  
REQ‑MECH‑004  Frame shall tolerate vibration profile defined in Testing domain.  
REQ‑MECH‑005  All load‑bearing fasteners shall be torque‑specified and documented.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. ELECTRICAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑ELEC‑001  System shall segment servo, compute, and accessory power rails.  
REQ‑ELEC‑002  BMS shall enforce over‑current protection.  
REQ‑ELEC‑003  BMS shall enforce under‑voltage protection.  
REQ‑ELEC‑004  Kill‑Line shall disable servo rail within latency ≤ 250 ms.  
REQ‑ELEC‑005  System shall prevent reverse polarity connection.  
REQ‑ELEC‑006  Duet 3 Mainboard shutdown shall engage automatic servo disable on Kill‑Line open.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. CONTROL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑CTRL‑001  System shall implement layered control authority model.  
REQ‑CTRL‑002  Reactive layer shall override Mission commands when safety violated.  
REQ‑CTRL‑003  Heartbeat timeout shall result in deterministic Safe‑Stop state.  
REQ‑CTRL‑004  System shall log authority layer transitions and timeouts.  
REQ‑CTRL‑005  No motion shall occur without validated Reactive authority.  
REQ‑CTRL‑006  Duet Bridge interface shall verify periodic heartbeat at ≥ 50 Hz rate.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑SAFE‑001  Hardware Emergency Stop (E‑Stop) shall directly interrupt servo rail.  
REQ‑SAFE‑002  Watchdog shall independently monitor Reactive and Mission heartbeats.  
REQ‑SAFE‑003  System shall default to STOP upon authority ambiguity.  
REQ‑SAFE‑004  Software shall not override Kill‑Line or BMS cutoff.  
REQ‑SAFE‑005  Operation shall cease below defined critical energy threshold.  
REQ‑SAFE‑006  Duet 3 outputs shall follow Kill‑Line state without delay > 100 ms.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. NAVIGATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑NAV‑001  System shall classify terrain before traversal.  
REQ‑NAV‑002  System shall refuse traversal of hazardous terrain classes.  
REQ‑NAV‑003  Navigation planner shall incorporate energy‑aware constraints.  
REQ‑NAV‑004  System shall perform controlled docking within ±15 mm tolerance.  
REQ‑NAV‑005  System shall maintain slope traversal ≤ specified in Deployment Profile.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. ENERGY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑ENER‑001  System shall estimate state‑of‑charge using coulomb count + voltage correlation.  
REQ‑ENER‑002  System shall initiate mission termination at defined SOC threshold (15 %).  
REQ‑ENER‑003  System shall avoid BMS hard cutoff during motion.  
REQ‑ENER‑004  System shall log battery temperature and current excursions.  
REQ‑ENER‑005  Charging shall not occur during active mission state.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑SW‑001  All motion commands shall include unique sequence identifier.  
REQ‑SW‑002  All safety‑critical messages must include timestamp and checksum.  
REQ‑SW‑003  System shall reject stale or malformed motion commands.  
REQ‑SW‑004  Message definitions and APIs shall be version‑controlled.  
REQ‑SW‑005  System shall log critical faults persistently and non‑volatilely.  
REQ‑SW‑006  Duet Bridge node shall publish status and error topics at ≥ 10 Hz.  

------------------------------------------------------------
10. COMMUNICATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑COMM‑001  Remote control channel shall be encrypted (TLS 1.3 minimum).  
REQ‑COMM‑002  Unauthorized command attempts shall be rejected and logged.  
REQ‑COMM‑003  Loss of remote link shall trigger Reactive Safe‑Stop.  
REQ‑COMM‑004  System shall display current authority layer externally.  
REQ‑COMM‑005  Swarm commands shall never override local safety authority.  
REQ‑COMM‑006  All Duet Bridge communications shall use checksum‑verified packets.  

------------------------------------------------------------
11. OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑OPS‑001  System shall operate within defined environmental envelope (−10 °C – 40 °C).  
REQ‑OPS‑002  Pre‑deployment checklist validation required before arm.  
REQ‑OPS‑003  System shall log mission duration and fault events per cycle.  
REQ‑OPS‑004  Manual Reset required after any E‑Stop event.  
REQ‑OPS‑005  Preventive maintenance intervals shall be documented and enforced.  

------------------------------------------------------------
12. LIFECYCLE REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

REQ‑LIFE‑001  All production units shall be serialized.  
REQ‑LIFE‑002  Engineering changes require documented impact analysis and approval.  
REQ‑LIFE‑003  Calibration records shall be stored per unit.  
REQ‑LIFE‑004  Decommissioned units shall be flagged in fleet registry.  
REQ‑LIFE‑005  All system documentation shall be revision‑controlled.   

------------------------------------------------------------
13. VERIFICATION TRACEABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Each requirement maps to:  
- Design Document (ID / Path)  
- Test Protocol (ID / Procedure)  
- Validation Result (record / date)  

No requirement may remain unverified prior to Phase 2 exit.  
Entries maintained in /09_Testing/Verification_Traceability_Matrix.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
14. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

This specification establishes measurable and testable system‑level requirements.  
All architecture, code, testing, and lifecycle documentation must maintain traceability.  
Safety and deterministic behavior remain non‑negotiable core principles.
