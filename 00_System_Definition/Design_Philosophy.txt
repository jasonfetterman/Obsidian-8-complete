1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define guiding principles, architectural priorities,
and engineering mindset for the design, development,
and deployment of OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.

The philosophy ensures:
- Safety‑first engineering
- Predictable system behavior
- Traceable and auditable changes
- Operational reliability
- Modular and upgradeable architecture
- Security subordinate to safety yet mandatory for deployment

------------------------------------------------------------
2. DESIGN PRINCIPLES
------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Safety Dominance  
Hardware and reactive control always take precedence
over software or network commands.  
Kill‑line, watchdog, and reactive safety layers cannot
be overridden by mission or swarm instructions.  
Reference /00_System_Definition/Safety_Architecture.txt  

2.2 Modularity and Extensibility  
Subsystems remain loosely coupled through defined
electrical, mechanical, and software interfaces.  
Hardware and software modules may be upgraded independently.  
Swappable sensors and actuators must never compromise baseline safety.

2.3 Deterministic Control  
System responses must be predictable under all expected conditions.  
Real‑time behavior is prioritized over non‑critical processing.  
Control loops include continuous monitoring, fault containment,
and fallback mechanisms.

2.4 Security Integration  
Cybersecurity measures protect communication, credentials,
and firmware integrity.  
Security controls remain subordinate to reactive safety systems.  
Threat modeling and validation guide secure design decisions.  
Reference /00_System_Definition/Cybersecurity_Threat_Model.txt  

2.5 Reliability and Redundancy  
Critical subsystems include redundant paths or fail‑safe modes.  
Hardware and software self‑monitor for faults or degradation.  
All failures must remain contained and recoverable.

2.6 Traceability and Auditability  
Every change to hardware, software, or calibration is tracked via
Configuration Management (CM) procedures.  
Each deployed unit maintains a validated Deployment_Profile and record
linking firmware tag, baseline revision, and calibration version.  
Reference /00_System_Definition/Configuration_Management_Plan.txt  

2.7 Human‑Centered Interaction  
Operator interfaces prioritize clarity, authority transparency,
and minimal cognitive load.  
All alerts and logs must be unambiguous and actionable.
Reference /03_Control_Architecture/System_Control_Authority_Model.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. ENGINEERING MINDSET
------------------------------------------------------------

Anticipate failure — design proactively to mitigate risk.  
Validate continuously — testing and verification at every stage.  
Maintain discipline — enforce CM, FMEA, and security protocols.  
Iterate responsibly — introduce improvements through controlled CRs and baselines.  
Respect context — environment, payload, and mission profile govern design trade‑offs.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

The OBSIDIAN‑8 V3 design philosophy ensures:
- Predictable and safe evolution of all units  
- Preservation of safety hierarchy and authority chain  
- Contained and analyzable failure behavior  
- Traceable software‑hardware‑network interaction  
- Security without safety compromise  
- Maintainable, modular, and field‑upgradeable architecture
