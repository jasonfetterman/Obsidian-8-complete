------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define estimated mass distribution and target
center‑of‑gravity (Cg) zone for frame validation,
stability control, and kinematic simulation alignment.  
Reference: /01_Mechanical/Frame_Architecture.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. STRUCTURAL MASS ESTIMATE
------------------------------------------------------------

3030 Aluminum Extrusion — Total Length ≈ 10.7 m  
Mass ≈ 1.8 – 2.0 kg/m → Primary structure ≈ 19 – 21 kg  

2020 Aluminum Extrusion — Total Length ≈ 7 m  
Mass ≈ 0.8 – 1.0 kg/m → Secondary structure ≈ 6 – 7 kg  

Printed CF Nylon Components ≈ 2 – 3 kg  
Fasteners + Hardware ≈ 2 kg  

Frame‑Only Mass ≈ 29 – 33 kg  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. PROJECTED FULL SYSTEM MASS
------------------------------------------------------------

Additions: Propulsion / Actuation System + Electronics + Power System + Payload  
Projected All‑Up Mass (AUM): 40 – 55 kg target  
Structural Design Maximum: ≤ 60 kg  
(Validated in /09_Testing/Load_Test_Plan.txt)  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. CENTER OF GRAVITY TARGET ZONE
------------------------------------------------------------

Horizontal Cg Offset ≤ ± 50 mm from geometric center.  
Vertical Cg = 35 % – 55 % of Frame Height.  

For FRAME_HEIGHT = 900 mm: Cg target 315 – 495 mm above base ring.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Cg CONTROL RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Mount dense components low when possible.  
- Place battery packs below mid‑height.  
- Promote symmetrical mass distribution about center plane.  
- Avoid cantilevered loads beyond ring perimeter.  
- Document any Cg shift when payloads exceed ± 5 % nominal mass.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. STATIC STABILITY CONDITION
------------------------------------------------------------

Projected Cg shall remain inside primary ring under 10° tilt.  
No tipping moment permitted under any static deployment.  
Validation geometry stored in /01_Mechanical/Static_Stability_Model.step  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. Cg VALIDATION METHOD
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Assemble frame and install component simulators.  
2. Suspend system from a single lift point.  
3. Mark vertical path from suspension point.  
4. Verify that path intersects central 100 mm diameter circle.  

Recorded photometric evidence is archived under /09_Testing/Cg_Validation/.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. DYNAMIC STABILITY CONSIDERATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Lower Cg improves: torsional resistance, landing stability, and vibration control.  
Excessively low Cg increases landing shock on base structure.  
Preferred vertical distribution maintains Cg at mid‑range for optimal response.  

Dynamic validation uses /03_Control_Architecture/Stability_Enforcer.txt parameters.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Balanced mass distribution enhances stability and energy efficiency.  
The defined Cg zone supports predictable motion isolation and safe stance behavior.  
All revisions to hardware or payload must update this model and associated simulations.
