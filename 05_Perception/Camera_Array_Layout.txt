1. PURPOSE

Define the Camera Array Layout for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Provides 360° situational awareness
Supports navigation, obstacle detection, and mission-specific vision tasks
Integrates with perception stack, autonomy, and control architecture
Ensures deterministic coverage and redundancy
2. CAMERA ARRAY CONFIGURATION
Camera ID	Position / Orientation	Resolution	Field of View (FOV)	Purpose
CAM-FRONT	Front-center, forward-facing	12 MP	90° horizontal	Forward obstacle detection, navigation
CAM-RIGHT	Right lateral, angled 45° forward	12 MP	90° horizontal	Lateral obstacle detection, mapping
CAM-LEFT	Left lateral, angled 45° forward	12 MP	90° horizontal	Lateral obstacle detection, mapping
CAM-REAR	Rear-center, backward-facing	12 MP	90° horizontal	Rear collision avoidance
CAM-TOP	Top-mounted, downward-facing	8 MP	120° vertical	Terrain mapping, stair detection
CAM-DIAG1	Front-right diagonal, 30° down	8 MP	60°	Stereo vision, depth estimation
CAM-DIAG2	Front-left diagonal, 30° down	8 MP	60°	Stereo vision, depth estimation
3. MOUNTING AND COVERAGE
Cameras mounted on rigid frame sections with vibration damping
Lens aligned with forward kinematic axes for consistent perception
Redundant overlap ensures no blind spots in primary navigation directions
Top and diagonal cameras provide terrain awareness and stereo depth
4. INTERFACES
Cameras connected via high-speed MIPI CSI or USB 3.0 depending on sensor type
Feeds processed by Mission Compute and Perception Compute Unit
ROS2 topics expose frames to autonomy stack and obstacle detection algorithms
Synchronization: hardware trigger ensures consistent timestamping for stereo/depth calculations
5. CALIBRATION AND ALIGNMENT
Intrinsic calibration for each camera (focal length, distortion, principal point)
Extrinsic calibration to define relative pose between cameras and frame
Calibration data stored in configuration repository, linked to deployment profile
Recalibration required after mechanical modification or sensor replacement
6. FAILSAFE AND REDUNDANCY
Overlapping FOV ensures continued perception if a single camera fails
Missing camera frame → autonomy stack switches to redundant data source
Critical navigation tasks rely on primary front stereo pair, with fallback lateral coverage
Logs all camera failures and switches for audit
7. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Verify field-of-view coverage in all directions
Test obstacle detection accuracy with all active cameras
Validate depth estimation and stereo processing under varied lighting
Perform vibration, shock, and thermal stress tests on mounts and connectors
8. DESIGN INTENT
Provide robust, redundant perception for safe autonomous operation
Ensure complete coverage for navigation, obstacle avoidance, and mission execution
Integrate seamlessly with autonomy, control, and sensor fusion layers
Maintain deterministic performance and fully auditable configuration
