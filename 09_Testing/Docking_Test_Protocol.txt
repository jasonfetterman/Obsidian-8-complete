1. PURPOSE

Define the Docking Test Protocol (DTP) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Verifies physical docking alignment, engagement, and disengagement of units with docking stations or other robots
Ensures collision avoidance, accuracy, and repeatability
Validates integration with perception, navigation, and reactive control systems
Provides pass/fail criteria for acceptance testing
2. TEST OBJECTIVES
Confirm docking alignment within specified tolerance
Validate engagement and latch mechanisms
Ensure safe disengagement without structural damage
Test system response to misalignment or obstacle interference
Confirm integration with perception and navigation for autonomous docking
Verify logging and telemetry capture for audit
3. TEST SETUP
Environment: controlled lab or designated test field
Docking Target: standard docking station or partner robot
Sensors Required: cameras, LiDAR, IMU, proximity sensors
Software Profile: FIELD_STANDARD deployment with Docking subsystem active
Logging: all ROS2 topics, actuator feedback, and event logs recorded
4. TEST PROCEDURE

Step 1 – Initialization:

Power up robot and docking station
Verify system health: actuators, sensors, energy, and communication

Step 2 – Approach:

Execute autonomous approach trajectory to docking target
Monitor sensor data to detect position and orientation relative to dock
Verify reactive safety layer prevents collisions

Step 3 – Alignment:

Engage precision alignment using perception system
Confirm angular and translational alignment within ±5 mm and ±2°

Step 4 – Engagement:

Activate docking mechanism
Verify mechanical engagement sensors indicate full latch
Monitor actuator currents and forces for anomalies

Step 5 – Stability Check:

Maintain docked state for 10–30 seconds
Check structural stability and sensor integrity
Verify energy and communication subsystems remain operational

Step 6 – Disengagement:

Command autonomous disengagement
Confirm smooth separation without collision or excessive force
Log all actuator feedback and docking status

Step 7 – Misalignment Test:

Repeat approach with intentional offsets of ±10 mm or ±5°
Verify system detects misalignment and safely aborts or corrects

Step 8 – Data Collection & Analysis:

Compile all sensor logs, ROS2 topics, and actuator feedback
Compare alignment, forces, and timing to acceptance thresholds
5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Docking alignment within ±5 mm translation and ±2° rotation
Engagement latch confirmed on first attempt ≥95% of trials
Disengagement occurs smoothly with no collisions or structural stress
Misalignment detection triggers safe abort or correction ≥100% of trials
All relevant logs captured and timestamped
6. FAILSAFE AND MONITORING
Kill-line or emergency stop immediately halts docking if unsafe conditions detected
Watchdog service monitors actuator responsiveness
All safety overrides logged for post-test audit
7. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure reliable, repeatable, and safe docking under autonomous and remote-controlled operation
Validate integration of mechanical, perception, navigation, and control subsystems
Maintain auditability, traceability, and reproducibility of test results
Provide clear acceptance metrics for deployment readiness
