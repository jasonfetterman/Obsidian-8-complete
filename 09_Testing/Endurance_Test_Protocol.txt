1. PURPOSE

Define the Endurance Test Protocol (ETP) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Evaluates long-duration system operation under mission-representative conditions
Validates energy management, thermal behavior, actuator reliability, and software stability
Ensures predictable system behavior and safety over extended periods
Provides objective criteria for acceptance and operational readiness
2. TEST OBJECTIVES
Verify continuous operation over mission duration (nominal: 8–12 hours, extended: up to 24 hours)
Validate energy system performance and SOC accuracy
Monitor actuator wear, thermal limits, and frame stress
Confirm software stability, ROS2 node uptime, and communication reliability
Ensure safety systems (kill-line, watchdog, reactive control) function consistently
Capture logs and telemetry for audit and post-test analysis
3. TEST SETUP
Environment: controlled lab or outdoor test field
Energy Profile: fully charged battery (LiFePO4 or LiPo depending on deployment profile)
Sensors & Monitoring: IMU, temperature sensors, energy meters, actuator feedback
Deployment Profile: FIELD_STANDARD or ENERGY_SAVER as appropriate
Logging: all ROS2 topics, actuator telemetry, and watchdog logs
4. TEST PROCEDURE

Step 1 – Initialization:

Power up system and verify readiness of all subsystems
Confirm software deployment profile and baseline configuration
Initialize data logging and telemetry

Step 2 – Continuous Operation:

Execute repetitive mission tasks (navigation, gaits, perception routines)
Maintain nominal speed and sensor operation
Record energy consumption, actuator currents, and temperature data

Step 3 – Periodic Checks:

Every hour: verify battery SOC matches predicted values
Inspect actuator temperatures and servo currents
Check ROS2 node health and kill-line responsiveness

Step 4 – Simulated Stress Events:

Introduce intermittent network loss to test autonomous behavior
Apply temporary high-load tasks to stress actuators
Observe reactive safety system response

Step 5 – Completion:

Safely power down system at end of test duration
Verify energy remaining, thermal limits, and actuator conditions
Archive logs, telemetry, and performance metrics
5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Continuous operation achieved for target duration without critical fault
SOC accuracy within ±3% over entire test
Actuator temperature below safety threshold for 100% of test
No ROS2 node crashes or unhandled exceptions
Kill-line and reactive safety remain fully operational throughout
All logs captured and verified for audit
6. FAILSAFE AND MONITORING
Watchdog service monitors system responsiveness at all times
Kill-line activated immediately if any critical fault occurs
Thermal or energy thresholds trigger safe slowdown or shutdown
Logs include all override events and emergency stop activations
7. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure long-duration reliability, energy predictability, and safety
Validate system integration across mechanical, electrical, software, perception, and navigation layers
Provide objective and auditable criteria for operational readiness
Confirm robustness under normal, high-load, and degraded network conditions
Maintain traceable records for continuous improvement and fleet deployment
