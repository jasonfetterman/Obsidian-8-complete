1. PURPOSE

Define the Movement Test Protocol (MTP) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Validates robot locomotion accuracy, gait stability, and actuator performance
Ensures reactive safety, energy-aware navigation, and control authority operate correctly
Provides measurable acceptance criteria for deployment readiness
2. TEST OBJECTIVES
Verify nominal gait execution (tripod, stair, and neutral stance)
Measure position and trajectory accuracy
Validate actuator response and torque delivery
Test reactive control under disturbances
Evaluate energy usage during continuous movement
Confirm integration with perception and navigation modules
Capture logs for post-test analysis
3. TEST SETUP
Environment: controlled lab, flat test surface, or standard test course
Deployment Profile: FIELD_STANDARD, PERFORMANCE_MODE, or ENERGY_SAVER
Sensors & Monitoring: IMU, encoder feedback, force sensors, ROS2 topics
Logging: all actuator telemetry, ROS2 command/feedback messages, and energy data
4. TEST PROCEDURE

Step 1 – Initialization:

Power up system, confirm actuator readiness
Verify deployment profile and baseline configuration
Activate ROS2 logging

Step 2 – Neutral Stance Test:

Command neutral stance
Record joint positions and stability metrics
Verify center of gravity within ±2 mm of nominal

Step 3 – Tripod Gait Test:

Execute forward and reverse tripod gait over 10 m course
Measure positional deviation vs. commanded path
Record joint torque and velocity profiles

Step 4 – Stair/Obstacle Test:

Command stair-climbing or obstacle negotiation gait
Measure lift, clearance, and joint synchronization
Verify no collisions or unsafe actuator stress

Step 5 – Disturbance Injection:

Apply external force or push perturbations
Verify reactive control maintains balance
Record recovery time and trajectory correction

Step 6 – Energy Monitoring:

Track SOC consumption and peak current
Verify energy-aware navigation adjusts speed or torque as expected

Step 7 – Continuous Loop Test:

Repeat gaits over extended distance (50–100 m)
Monitor actuator temperature and ROS2 node stability
Capture all telemetry for audit
5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Positional deviation ≤ 2% of path length
Joint torque within ±5% of commanded limits
Recovery from disturbance within 1 s, maintaining stability
Energy consumption matches predicted deployment profile ±5%
No actuator faults, node crashes, or kill-line activations
All logs complete and verified
6. FAILSAFE AND MONITORING
Watchdog monitors system responsiveness and triggers kill-line if needed
Reactive safety layer prevents tipping, overextension, or unsafe motion
Continuous actuator and energy monitoring for threshold violations
Emergency stop available at all times
7. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure accurate, safe, and reliable locomotion under all test scenarios
Validate integration of mechanical, electrical, control, perception, and energy systems
Provide measurable and auditable metrics for acceptance
Confirm robustness of reactive safety and authority control
Maintain traceable records for fleet deployment readiness
