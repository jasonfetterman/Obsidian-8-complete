1. PURPOSE

Define the neutral stance geometry for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.3 (hybrid extrusion + 3D printed frame).  
This stance is the baseline posture used for:

- Calibration and sensor alignment
- Actuator range verification
- Center of gravity determination
- Simulation and control authority testing
- Deployment and maintenance reference

2. REFERENCE FRAME

Origin: Center of 3030 backbone base plate (0,0,0)  
Axes:  
X: Forward  
Y: Lateral (right positive)  
Z: Upward  
Units: Meters (m) and degrees (°)

3. LIMB POSITIONS AND ANGLES

Limb / Joint                 Position XYZ (m)   Rotation (deg)   Notes
Front Left Leg (FL) – Hip    0.35, 0.25, 0.42  0               Neutral hip orientation
Front Left Leg – Knee         0.35, 0.25, 0.22  30              Slight bend for weight support
Front Left Leg – Ankle        0.35, 0.25, 0.05  0               Foot flat on ground
Front Right Leg (FR) – Hip    0.35, -0.25, 0.42 0               Symmetric to FL
Front Right Leg – Knee        0.35, -0.25, 0.22 30
Front Right Leg – Ankle       0.35, -0.25, 0.05 0
Rear Left Leg (RL) – Hip      -0.35, 0.25, 0.42 0
Rear Left Leg – Knee          -0.35, 0.25, 0.22 30
Rear Left Leg – Ankle         -0.35, 0.25, 0.05 0
Rear Right Leg (RR) – Hip     -0.35, -0.25, 0.42 0
Rear Right Leg – Knee         -0.35, -0.25, 0.22 30
Rear Right Leg – Ankle        -0.35, -0.25, 0.05 0

Notes: Knee bend angles chosen for static stability; foot flat on ground for CG alignment; Z coordinates lowered slightly from V3.2 to account for extrusion base and printed brackets.

4. TORSO AND PAYLOAD ALIGNMENT

- Torso base plate level with horizontal plane (Z = 0.42 m)  
- Mission compute and battery modules centered along X-axis (longitudinal) and Y = 0 (lateral)  
- Sensor mounts oriented parallel to chassis top plane  
- Armor panels installed in neutral stance for clearance verification

5. CENTER OF GRAVITY ALIGNMENT

Neutral stance aligns CG directly above the midpoint of the base footprint:  
CG X: 0.0 m  
CG Y: 0.0 m  
CG Z: 0.42 m  

Ensures minimum tipping moment during static calibration and initial actuation.  
Lighter 3D printed limbs slightly lower vertical CG while maintaining stable polygon.

6. DESIGN INTENT

- Provides baseline posture for calibration, simulation, and motion planning  
- Ensures symmetry across all limbs for stability and accurate CG computation  
- Supports integration with Mass_and_CG_Model, Actuation Systems, and Control Authority Model  
- Reference for FMEA testing and neutral load verification  
- Compatible with hybrid extrusion + printed frame for modular maintenance
