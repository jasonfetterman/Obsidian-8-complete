1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the neutral stance geometry for OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4
(hybrid 3030/2020 extrusion frame with 3D‑printed limbs and modules).  

This stance is the standardized baseline used for:
- Calibration and sensor alignment  
- Actuator range verification and torque profiling  
- Center‑of‑gravity (Cg) determination  
- Simulation, gait planning, and control‑authority testing  
- Deployment and maintenance reference configuration  

Reference documents: /01_Mechanical/Mass_and_CG_Model.txt and /03_Control_Architecture/Stability_Enforcer.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. REFERENCE FRAME
------------------------------------------------------------

Origin: center of 3030 backbone base plate (0, 0, 0)  
Axes:  X = Forward Y = Lateral (right positive) Z = Upward  
Units: Meters (m) and Degrees (°)  

All coordinates based on assembled frame V3 baseline geometry (/00_System_Definition/Geometry_Model.txt).  

------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------
4. TORSO AND PAYLOAD ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Torso base plate aligned with horizontal (X–Y) plane at Z = 0.42 m.  
- Mission compute and battery modules centered along X axis; Y = 0 (lateral neutral).  
- Sensor mounts oriented parallel to chassis top plane.  
- Armor panels installed in neutral pose to verify clearances and mount compression.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. CENTER OF GRAVITY ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Neutral stance aligns Cg directly above the base footprint midpoint.  

Nominal Cg:   X = 0.00 m Y = 0.00 m Z = 0.42 m  

Cg Verification Procedure:  
1. Suspend unit from hip mount and mark vertical through center.  
2. Line must intersect with 100 mm diameter circle around origin.  
3. Regain balance without servo assistance within ± 2 s settle time.  

Lighter printed limbs decrease vertical Cg ≈ 0.01 m while maintaining polygon stability.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. VALIDATION LINKAGES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Neutral geometry used as zero‑position for Tripod_Gait_Kinematics simulation.  
- Forms baseline for Calibration Configuration (/09_Testing/Calibration_Sequence.txt).  
- Cross‑referenced by Stair_Mode_Mechanics for stair entry/exit alignment.  
- Employed during FMEA tests for neutral load verification (FM‑001 – FM‑005).  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Provide symmetrical reference for control and calibration across limbs.  
- Ensure predictable Cg and support polygon under zero‑motion state.  
- Permit direct integration with Mass_and_CG_Model, Actuation Systems, and Control Authority Model.  
- Guarantee repeatable neutral condition for deployment and maintenance alignment.  
- Support hybrid extrusion + printed frame architecture for fast service and interchangeability.
