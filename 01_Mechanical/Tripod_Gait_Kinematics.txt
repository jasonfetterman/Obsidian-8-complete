1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the kinematic behavior and control parameters for tripod gait locomotion of OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4.

This model ensures:
- Stable and efficient flat‑terrain movement.  
- Integration with Reactive Safety, Control Authority, and Neutral Stance Geometry.  
- Predictable Cg tracking and actuator coordination for control simulation and mission execution.  

References: /01_Mechanical/Neutral_Stance_Geometry.txt  /03_Control_Architecture/System_Control_Authority_Model.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. GAIT DEFINITION
------------------------------------------------------------

**Tripod Gait** = alternating movement of two sets of three limbs while maintaining ≥ 3 points of ground contact.

**Limb Sets:**  
- Set A = Front Left (FL), Rear Right (RR), optional Middle (if configured)  
- Set B = Front Right (FR), Rear Left (RL), optional Middle (if configured)  

Cycle Sequence:  
A‑lift → advance forward → A‑down → B‑lift → advance → B‑down → repeat.  

Contact balance model ensures support polygon stability throughout Phase Shift 180°.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. STEP PARAMETERS
------------------------------------------------------------

| Parameter | Nominal Value | Notes |
|:--|:--:|:--|
| Step Length (X‑axis) | 0.25 m | Safe range without over‑extension |
| Step Height (Z‑axis) | 0.05 m | Neutral terrain clearance |
| Swing Duration | 0.5 s | Time limb is elevated |
| Stance Duration | 0.5 s | Time limb maintains contact |
| Duty Factor | 0.50 | Stance / Swing ratio for tripod stability |

Parameters adapt automatically to payload (+/‑ 2 kg) and terrain incline (< 7°).  
Dynamic Cg controller adjusts setpoint continuously during transition phases.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. LIMB KINEMATICS
------------------------------------------------------------

| Joint | Neutral Angle (°) | Swing Adjustment (°) | Notes |
|:--|:--:|:--:|:--|
| Hip | 0 | ±15 | Forward / back translation vector |
| Knee | 30 | ±20 | Lift for terrain clearance |
| Ankle | 0 | ±10 | Foot surface alignment |

Synchronization between A/B limb sets maintains continuous tripod support polygon.  
Reactive controller modulates joint angles to follow torque and sensor feedback.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. STABILITY CONSIDERATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

- ≥ 3 ground contact points maintained at all times.  
- Cg shift actively compensated along X / Y axes to prevent tip‑over moments.  
- Torque limits held ≤ 80 % of rated continuous output to protect actuators.  
- IMU and ground‑force sensors detect slippage / imbalance within 50 ms window.  
- Reactive Safety overrides motion if tilt > 10° or contact loss > 1 leg.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. CONTROL INTEGRATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Tripod Gait Controller = Mission Compute Layer (primary) + Reactive Safety (preemptive).  

Authority Chain:  
1. Hardware Kill‑Line / Watchdog  
2. Reactive Safety Layer (collision & stability priority)  
3. Mission Gait Controller (trajectory planning)  
4. Remote / Swarm commands (read‑only while mid‑stride)  

Failsafe: hardware kill and watchdog can terminate sequence instantly.  
Swarm/remote input cannot interrupt mid‑step unless Safe‑Stop authenticated.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. VALIDATION AND TESTING
------------------------------------------------------------

**Simulation Tests**  
- Flat & inclined terrain (±5°) with payload variation.  
- Gait speed profile: 0.2 – 0.5 m/s.  
- Performance metrics: step length accuracy, Cg deviation, torque usage, thermal response.  

**Field Tests**  
- Concrete, carpet, and packed dirt substrates.  
- Incremental load cases from nominal to +20 %.  

FMEA Cross‑References: FM‑003 (Battery Undervoltage), FM‑004 (Servo Overheat), FM‑008 (IMU Drift).  
Results archived in /09_Testing/Tripod_Gait_Validation_Report.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Provide predictable, repeatable tripod gait with hardware‑dominant safety.  
- Maintain stable motion and torque‑balanced distribution for flat terrain.  
- Integrate seamlessly with Neutral Stance, Mass/Cg Model, Stair‑Mode Mechanics, and Control Authority.  
- Enable simulation, calibration, and field deployment of reliable gait patterns.
