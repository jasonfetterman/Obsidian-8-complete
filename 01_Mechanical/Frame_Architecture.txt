1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the mechanical frame architecture of OBSIDIAN‑8 V3
employing a hybrid aluminum‑extrusion and 3D‑printed modular design.
Covers structural layout, load paths, material selection, interfaces,
and integration with actuators, sensors, armor, and routing systems.

This document ensures:
- Predictable mechanical behavior under operational loads  
- Compatibility with printed subsystems and armor modules  
- Modularity for maintenance and upgrade iteration  
- Traceable design decisions linked to Configuration Management records  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. FRAME STRUCTURE OVERVIEW
------------------------------------------------------------

Main Chassis:  
- Backbone constructed from 3030 aluminum extrusions forming the central torso structure.  
- Primary load path for battery, mission compute, and limb attachments.  

Secondary Supports:  
- 2020 extrusions form cross‑braces, sensor mounts, and payload bay frames.  
- Provide rigidity while minimizing mass.  

Leg Attachments:  
- Modular 3D‑printed interface brackets connect limbs to 3030 backbone.  
- Compatible with Leg_MK1_Obsidian and Leg_MK2_Obsidian assemblies.  
- High‑stress points reinforced with gussets and CNC brackets.  

Torso Modules:  
- 3D‑printed housings for Mission Compute, Battery System, and Sensor arrays.  
- Slide‑in or bolt‑on mounts for quick access and service.  

Armor Mounting Points:  
- Quick‑release printed brackets align with extrusion slots.  
- Panels (Covers_armor) field‑replaceable without tools.  

Payload Bays:  
- Custom add‑on compartments mount within extrusion framework.  
- Reconfigurable per mission requirement.  

Maintenance Access:  
- Slide‑fit or bolted removal panels; no backbone disassembly required.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. MATERIAL SELECTION AND PROPERTIES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Component | Material / Method | Notes / Purpose |
|:-----------|:------------------|:----------------|
| Main Chassis | 3030 Aluminum Extrusions | High stiffness / modular load core |
| Secondary Supports | 2020 Aluminum Extrusions | Low mass cross‑brace structure |
| Limb Brackets | CF Nylon or PETG (printed) | Strength at actuator mounts / customizable |
| Armor Panels | Printed + Polycarbonate Hybrid | Impact resilience and light weight |
| Internal Panels | ABS/PETG Printed Sheets | Lightweight and removable |
| Fasteners | M5/M6 Bolts + T‑slot Nuts | Standardized mount interface |
| Gaskets/Pads | Rubber or TPU prints | Vibration damping at actuator joints |

------------------------------------------------------------
4. LOAD AND STRESS CONSIDERATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

- Backbone extrusions support both dynamic motion and static loading.  
- Global safety factor ≥ 2.0 for all extrusion members and 3D‑printed mounts.  
- Critical joints (leg mounts, shoulders) reinforced with metal brackets.  
- FEA validation required on main 3030 torso and actuator bracket geometry.  
- Vibration damping via printed gaskets or rubber pads at high‑frequency mounts.  
- Thermal expansion tolerance maintained ≤ 0.25 mm across 1 m span.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. MODULARITY AND INTERFACES
------------------------------------------------------------

Actuator Interfaces:  
- 3D‑printed mounting plates bolt to extrusion end‑plates.  
- Slot geometry enables ± 3 mm alignment tuning.  
- Torque limits defined per actuator type in Actuation_Systems specs.  

Sensor Mounts:  
- 2020 extrusion‑mounted sliders with locking tabs enable orientation adjustment and rapid replacement.  

Armor Panels:  
- Quick‑release brackets linked to Covers_armor dataset; swappable without tools.  

Power and Data Channels:  
- Internal extrusion cavities route wiring; printed guides clip along rails for secure retention.  
- Supports CAN, 12 V, and 5 V low‑current bus lines to minimize external bundling.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. COMPATIBILITY WITH CM / DEPLOYMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Frame revision tracked in Configuration Management Plan (/00_System_Definition/Configuration_Management_Plan.txt).  
- Each unit links to Deployment_Profile specifying hardware and printed part revisions.  
- Structural or interface changes require approved Change Request (CR) under Class A or B as applicable.  
- FEA and test records must be attached to CR on approved revision.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Deliver high structural integrity with minimal mass through extrusion backbone.  
- Maintain fully modular and upgradeable layout compatible with printed limbs, sensors, and armor.  
- Ensure predictable response under dynamic load and articulation events.  
- Guarantee traceability and auditability for every mechanical change.  
- Support future integration of alternative materials (CF composite, light‑alloy) without geometry drift.
