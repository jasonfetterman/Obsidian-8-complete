1. PURPOSE

Define the mass properties and center of gravity (CG) model for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.3 (hybrid extrusion + 3D printed design).  
This ensures:

- Stability under operational motion
- Proper load distribution across limbs and actuators
- Accurate simulation for control and safety systems
- Traceability for CM, FMEA, and deployment profiles

2. TOTAL SYSTEM MASS

Subsystem                   Mass (kg)       Notes / Revision
Main Chassis (3030 + 2020) 8.5             Hybrid aluminum extrusion backbone
Actuated Legs (6x printed)  6.0             3D printed limbs MK1/MK2, reinforced brackets
Torso Modules (3D printed)  5.0             Battery, compute, sensors in printed housings
Armor Covers (printed + polycarbonate) 3.0  Modular panels, snap-on mounts
Sensors & Payload           1.5             Cameras, LIDAR, mission modules
Wiring & Harness            0.8             Routed through extrusion channels
Total System Mass           24.8            Rounded

Notes:
- Mass reduction achieved through hybrid design and lighter printed brackets
- Lighter legs reduce torque on actuators and improve stair clearance

3. CENTER OF GRAVITY (CG) MODEL

Origin: Geometric center of 3030 backbone reference frame

CG Location (Nominal, XYZ):
X: 0.0 m (longitudinal center)
Y: 0.0 m (lateral center)
Z: 0.42 m (height from extrusion base)

CG Sensitivity:
Payload addition ±0.08 m
Armor variation ±0.04 m
Battery placement ±0.02 m
Printed module variability ±0.03 m

CG Considerations:
- Legs designed to support >120% of static weight
- Torso and payload mass concentrated near CG to minimize torque on actuators
- Armor and add-ons symmetrically placed to maintain lateral balance
- Printed parts reduce mass high on the frame to lower vertical CG

4. DYNAMIC MASS CONSIDERATIONS

Actuation during motion changes effective CG:

Forward/Back Leg Extension: ±0.04 m X-axis  
Lateral Swing: ±0.025 m Y-axis  
Limb Elevation: ±0.015 m Z-axis  

Software control loops use this dynamic CG to:

- Adjust torque in real-time
- Maintain stability in reactive controller
- Feed FMEA risk models for motion limits

5. MODULARITY AND CONFIGURATION

- Each subsystem mass tracked in Configuration Management Plan
- Changes in battery, armor, or payload must update deployment_profile with new mass/CG data
- Supports rapid recalculation for mission-specific loadouts
- Modular 3D printed parts allow quick mass adjustment and replacement

6. DESIGN INTENT

- Maintain predictable stability and actuation performance under all operational configurations
- Ensure safe operation with both static and dynamic loads
- Integrate with FMEA, CM, and System Control Authority to prevent tipping, overload, or actuator faults
- Provide accurate data for simulation, mission planning, and control authority validation
- Reduce weight while keeping CG low for stair climbing and tripod gait stability
