1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the comprehensive mass and center‑of‑gravity (Cg)
model for OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4 (hybrid extrusion + 3D‑printed architecture).

This model ensures:
- Stability during all operational gaits and terrain conditions  
- Proper load distribution across limbs, actuators, and payloads  
- Accurate input for control and safety simulation loops  
- Traceability for Configuration Management (CM), FMEA, and deployment records  
Reference: /00_System_Definition/Geometry_Model.txt and /01_Mechanical/Neutral_Stance_Geometry.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. TOTAL SYSTEM MASS
------------------------------------------------------------

| Subsystem | Mass (kg) | Notes / Revision |
|:-----------|:-----------:|:----------------|
| Main Chassis (3030 + 2020) | 8.5 | Hybrid aluminum extrusion backbone |
| Actuated Legs (8 × printed) | 7.5 | 3D printed MK1/MK2 limbs + reinforced brackets |
| Torso Modules (3D printed) | 5.0 | Battery, mission compute, sensor bays |
| Armor Covers (printed + polycarbonate) | 3.0 | Snap‑on panel system |
| Sensors & Payload | 1.5 | RGB/Depth cameras, LIDAR, RTK components |
| Wiring & Harness | 0.8 | Internally routed through extrusions |
| Total System Mass | 26.3 | Target tolerance ± 0.5 kg |

Notes:
- Hybrid design achieves mass reduction without compromising strength.  
- Reduced leg mass minimizes servo torque demands during stair gait.  
- All values are baseline averages recorded after integration testing.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. CENTER OF GRAVITY (CG) MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

Origin: Geometric center of 3030 backbone reference frame  

Nominal Cg Location (XYZ m):  
X = 0.00 Y = 0.00 Z = 0.42 (above extrusion base)  

Cg Sensitivity (1 kg mass change):  
- Payload addition → ΔZ ≈ ± 0.08 m  
- Armor variation → ΔZ ≈ ± 0.04 m  
- Battery placement shift → ΔZ ≈ ± 0.02 m  
- Printed module variation → ΔZ ≈ ± 0.03 m  

Cg Considerations:
- Limb assemblies support > 120 % of static load.  
- Torso/payload mass kept around Cg column to reduce moment arm torque.  
- Armor symmetry maintains lateral balance (Y axis neutral).  
- Printed components reduce upper mass, lowering vertical Cg.  
- Neutral stands align Cg directly above base footprint midpoint (Z = 0.42 m).  

Reference: /01_Mechanical/Neutral_Stance_Geometry.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. DYNAMIC MASS CONSIDERATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Actuation changes effective Cg during motion:  
- Forward/Rear Leg Extension → ΔX ± 0.04 m  
- Lateral Swing → ΔY ± 0.025 m  
- Limb Elevation → ΔZ ± 0.015 m  

Reactive Controller and Mission Compute use dynamic Cg data to:  
- Adjust torque allocation in real time for balanced gait.  
- Maintain stability margins within Reactive Safety envelope.  
- Drive FMEA risk model inputs for motion threshold prediction.  

Reference: /03_Control_Architecture/Reactive_Controller_Spec.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. MODULARITY AND CONFIGURATION
------------------------------------------------------------

- Each subsystem mass recorded in Configuration Management Register.  
- Changes in battery, armor, or payload require Deployment_Profile update with new mass/Cg values.  
- Supports rapid mission‑specific re‑balancing via mass calculator tool.  
- Modular printed parts allow field replaceable mass adjustments.  
- Geometry offsets linked to Neutral_Stance_Geometry for load simulation accuracy.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. VALIDATION AND ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Cg and Mass Validation Steps:  
1. Verify torso plate alignment at Z = 0.42 m base plane.  
2. Check mission compute and battery modules centered (X = 0, Y = 0).  
3. Install sensor mounts parallel to chassis top plane.  
4. Mount armor panels in neutral stance to confirm clearance and mass symmetry.  
5. Record Cg deviation ≤ ± 10 mm using suspension test procedure.  

Dynamic verification performed during Tripod Gait Evaluation (/03_Control_Architecture/Gait_Control_Spec.txt).  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Maintain predictable stability and actuation performance across all loadouts.  
- Ensure safe operation under combined static and dynamic forces.  
- Integrate with FMEA and CM to prevent tipping, overload, or servo faults.  
- Provide accurate mass/Cg data for simulation and mission planning.  
- Keep overall mass as low as practical with Cg below mid‑frame for stair and tripod stability.
