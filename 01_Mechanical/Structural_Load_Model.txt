1. PURPOSE

Define mechanical load distribution and structural integrity for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures the frame and actuators handle static, dynamic, and impact loads.
Provides reference for FEA, CM, FMEA, and deployment validation.
Supports stair traversal, payload handling, and normal locomotion.
2. LOAD TYPES
Load Type	Description	Notes
Static Load	Weight of chassis, payload, battery, armor at rest	Base design load
Dynamic Load	Forces during walking, stair traversal, acceleration/deceleration	Up to 1.5× static
Impact Load	Sudden contact or collision	Safety factor ≥ 2.0
Torsional Load	Twist forces on frame due to uneven terrain	Evaluated per actuator mount
Bending Load	Beam bending on chassis and limb mounts	Monitored via FEA
3. LOAD DISTRIBUTION
Legs: Each leg designed to support 40–45% of total static mass during neutral stance.
Torso Modules: Evenly distribute battery and mission payload along X-axis to minimize torsion.
Armor Panels: Lightweight panels reduce uneven lateral loading.
Actuators: Torque limits enforce safe operation under dynamic loads.
Support Polygon: CG kept within polygon defined by limb contact points to prevent tipping.
4. SAFETY FACTORS AND MARGINS
Material Factor of Safety: ≥ 2.0 for all primary load-bearing elements.
Joint Stress Limit: 80% of yield strength under max expected load.
Deflection Limits:
Chassis backbone: ≤ 1.5 mm under max static load
Limb mounting plates: ≤ 2 mm under max dynamic load
5. LOAD CASES
Case ID	Scenario	Max Load (kg)	Notes
LC-001	Neutral stance, static	33.3	Base CG
LC-002	Forward motion, flat terrain	50	Includes dynamic torque
LC-003	Stair ascent, full payload	55	Step height 0.3 m
LC-004	Stair descent, full payload	55	Step height 0.3 m
LC-005	Sudden impact, frontal collision	70	Safety factor applied
All cases analyzed via Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to confirm stress limits, deflection, and safety margins.
6. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER MODELS
Links to Frame_Architecture.txt for mounting points.
Compatible with Mass_and_CG_Model.txt for load calculations.
Supports Neutral_Stance_Geometry.txt for baseline stance and CG reference.
Provides inputs for Stair_Mode_Mechanics.txt to calculate dynamic load shifts.
FMEA references: FM-004 (servo overheating), FM-005 (structural crack), FM-009 (power distribution stress).
7. DESIGN INTENT
Maintain structural integrity across all operational scenarios.
Minimize deflection and stress on critical components.
Ensure actuator and limb safety under dynamic and impact loads.
Provide predictable mechanical behavior for control authority and reactive safety systems.
Fully traceable and CM-compliant for all load-bearing elements.
