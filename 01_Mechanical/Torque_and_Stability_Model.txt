1. SYSTEM MASS ASSUMPTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Target Total Mass (Operational Configuration): ≈ 26 kg (hybrid extrusion + printed limbs)  

Gravitational Force:  F = m × g = 26 kg × 9.81 m/s² ≈ 255 N  

This represents the complete system weight applied across leg contact points in neutral stance.  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. STATIC TRIPOD SUPPORT MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

Tripod gait assumes three legs carry the entire system weight.  

Load per support leg = 255 N ÷ 3 ≈ 85 N  

Momentum and terrain factor = × 2 (over acceleration and uncertainty)  

**Design Load per Leg:** ≈ 170 N  

Minimum 3 points of contact maintained at all times (Tripod Gait standard).  
Cg adjusted dynamically through Reactive Controller to remain within support polygon.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. TIBIA TORQUE REQUIREMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Effective lever length (L): 0.18 m  (typical tibia segment length)  
Applied load (F): 170 N  

τ = F × L = 170 N × 0.18 m ≈ 30.6 Nm required per tibia joint under peak motion.  

Includes 1.2× dynamic correction for forward stride offset.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. SERVO CAPABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

Example Actuator: **JX PDI‑HV2060MG**  
Rated Torque: 60 kg·cm @ 7.4 V   

Conversion: 1 kg·cm = 0.098 Nm →  60 kg·cm ≈ 5.9 Nm  

=> Single servo cannot sustain 30 Nm directly if loaded at full lever arm.  

Therefore:  
- Mechanical geometry reduces required moment arm.  
- Torque is shared across Femur + Coxa when Cg centered.  
- Reactive controller prevents stall conditions.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Stability maintained through:
- Optimized leg geometry and motion limits (Coxa: ±60°, Femur: −30° → +75°, Tibia: −100° → +20°).  
- Reduced moment arm during high‑load moments.  
- Load sharing across three limbs (Tripod Gait Model).  
- Reactive control to adjust torque outputs by limb feedback.  
- Cg held inside triangle formed by contact legs (Z ≈ 0.42 m).  

Servo operation never at full extension while bearing maximum load.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. STAIR MODE TORQUE LOAD
------------------------------------------------------------

During stair ascent the front tripod absorbs majority of load as Cg moves forward.  

Dynamic Multiplier: 2.5 × static load  
Design load ≈ 212 N per front limb  

Torque @ 0.18 m = τ ≈ 38 Nm (max expected)  

Safety response:  
- Avoid sustained static hold at extension.  
- Reactive controller reduces stride frequency under high load.  
- If torque > threshold or temperature rise > limit → Fail‑safe halt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. STABILITY AND SUPPORT POLYGON MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

Cg projection maintained inside tripod support triangle through active compensation.  

Tilt limit: ≤ 10 ° sustained during stair climb or irregular terrain.  
Abort trigger: if Cg approaches polygon edge (< 5 mm margin) or leg slip detected.  

Force sensors and IMU cross‑check Cg vector against terrain slope each cycle (≥ 100 Hz update).  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. TORQUE AND SAFETY LOGIC
------------------------------------------------------------

System does not rely on stall torque capacity.  
Control framework implements:  
- Shortened moment arm profiles (geometry optimization).  
- Tripod gait scheduling with limited stride length on low voltage.  
- Proportional torque limiting with predictive thermal slack.  
- Reactive Safety preemption for over‑current and over‑temp signals.  
- Watchdog failsafe override to hardware Kill‑Line if safety flag asserted.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. CONTROL INTEGRATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Tripod Gait managed by Mission Compute Layer and preempted by Reactive Safety Controller if collision, slippage, or instability detected.  
Swarm / Remote commands cannot interrupt leg cycle mid‑step unless Safe‑Stop triggered.  

References: /03_Control_Architecture/System_Control_Authority_Model.txt and /01_Mechanical/Stair_Mode_Mechanics.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
10. VALIDATION AND METRICS
------------------------------------------------------------

Simulation: flat terrain, stairs, and variable payload conditions.  
Data recorded: step length accuracy, torque utilization, Cg deviation, actuator temperature, slip incidents.  
FMEA cross‑links: FM‑003 (Undervoltage), FM‑004 (Overheat), FM‑008 (IMU Drift), FM‑005 (Structural Crack).  

------------------------------------------------------------
11. UPGRADE PATH AND ENGINEERING NOTES
------------------------------------------------------------

If torque margin insufficient:  
- Adopt 80 – 100 kg·cm class servos (7.8 – 9.8 Nm nominal).  
- Introduce dual‑servo assist for Tibia actuation.  
- Reinforce printed brackets with aluminum bushings and carbon fiber overlays.  
- Recalculate torque envelope and update Mass / CG Model accordingly.  

------------------------------------------------------------
12. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Establish verified torque and stability envelope for tripod and stair operation.  
- Maintain predictable motion without servo overload or instability.  
- Support safe, repeatable behavior by tying mechanical limits to software control.  
- Ensure traceability to CM, FMEA, and Reactive Safety architecture.  

Movement is controlled, energy‑aware, and geometrically bounded for long‑term operational reliability.
