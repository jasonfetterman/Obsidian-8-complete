OBSIDIAN-8 V3
PROPULSION MOUNT GEOMETRY v3
Based on: V3.3_HYBRID_FRAME
Status: MOTOR STRUCTURAL INTERFACE DEFINITION
Units: Millimeters

---------------------------------------
PURPOSE
---------------------------------------

Define structural motor mounting geometry
and thrust/torque load transfer path.

Mount must:
- Transfer thrust directly into aluminum structure
- Avoid loading printed parts in primary tension
- Maintain radial and angular symmetry
- Support stair-mode and dynamic torque loads

---------------------------------------
PROPULSION LAYOUT
---------------------------------------

Configuration:
8 motor positions

Angular spacing:
Aligned with vertical posts (45° increments)

Radial position:
Primary ring center radius: 556.5 mm

Motor axis:
Vertical (Z+)

---------------------------------------
THRUST LOAD ASSUMPTION
---------------------------------------

Assumed maximum total thrust:
≥ 2 × all-up mass

Hybrid frame mass: 24.8–25 kg
Total thrust ≈ 500 N (system nominal)
Design margin per motor: 2×
≈ 125–150 N nominal, 300 N peak per motor

---------------------------------------
LOAD PATH
---------------------------------------

Motor Mount
→ Aluminum Motor Plate (5–8 mm)
→ 3030 Vertical Post
→ Primary Ring Node
→ Radial Bracing
→ Opposing structure

Printed components carry only shear stabilization.
No axial compression or torque allowed through nylon parts.

---------------------------------------
MOTOR MOUNT PLATE
---------------------------------------

Material:
Aluminum 5–8 mm thick (structural grade)

Mount pattern:
Bolt directly to vertical 3030 post
or reinforced bracket

Fasteners:
M6 or M8, Grade 10.9 minimum
Minimum 4 per motor, ≥ 30 mm spacing

---------------------------------------
TORQUE & THRUST RESISTANCE
---------------------------------------

Each motor produces:

- Vertical thrust
- Radial/axial torque due to offset load

Mount must resist:

- 2× nominal thrust per motor
- Reaction torque from stair-mode dynamics

Verification:

- Manual torque test: no visible flex of post
- FEM simulation: peak torsion < 0.5° at post node

---------------------------------------
CLEARANCE ZONES
---------------------------------------

Maintain:

- ≥ 50 mm internal buffer from primary ring inward
- No interference with:
  - Upper ring
  - Cross bracing
  - Core cylinder (300 mm diameter)
  - Landing interface foot blocks

---------------------------------------
VIBRATION & THERMAL CONSIDERATIONS
---------------------------------------

- Mounts must be rigid
- No soft isolation at motor-to-structure interface
- Vibration isolation occurs at electronics layer only
- Maintain 5–10 mm air gap between motor and CF nylon components to avoid heat transfer

---------------------------------------
ALIGNMENT TOLERANCES
---------------------------------------

- Vertical alignment < 0.5° deviation
- Radial symmetry ±2 mm
- Fasteners torque-verified post-install

---------------------------------------
FAILURE CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------

Redesign required if:

- Vertical post flex visible under 2× thrust simulation
- Post torsional twist exceeds 0.5° at motor mount
- Printed components deform under dynamic load
- Fasteners loosen after operational run
- Motor plate offsets > 2 mm from designed radial location
