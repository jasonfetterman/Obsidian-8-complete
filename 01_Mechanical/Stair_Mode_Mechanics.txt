1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define mechanical and kinematic behavior of OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4 during stair traversal using the hybrid 3030/2020 extrusion backbone and 3D‑printed limbs with reinforced brackets.  

This document ensures:
- Stable motion for ascending and descending stairs  
- Integration with Control Authority, Reactive Safety, and Neutral Stance Geometry  
- Reference for calibration, test development, and field deployment  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. MODE ACTIVATION CRITERIA
------------------------------------------------------------

**Stair mode engages when:**
- Front leg detects step elevation > 0.05 m (via LIDAR or proximity sensor)  
- Incline exceeds normal ramp threshold (≥ 8 ° slope)  
- Support polygon stability confirmed by Reactive Controller filter  

**Automatic exit conditions:**
- Measured step elevation < 0.02 m for > 1 s  
- Terrain returns to flat baseline or neutral stance regained  

Hysteresis control prevents frequent enable/disable cycling.  
All stair‑mode events logged in /09_Testing/Motion_Log.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. LEG KINEMATICS ADJUSTMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

| Joint | Nominal Angle | Stair Ascend Adj | Stair Descend Adj | Notes |
|:------|:--------------:|:----------------:|:------------------:|:--|
| Hip | 0 ° | +10 ° | −10 ° | Raises step height and fore reach. |
| Knee | 30 ° | +20 ° | +10 ° | Prevents scraping during transition. |
| Ankle | 0 ° | +5 ° | −5 ° | Aligns foot surface to step angle. |

Notes:  
- CF Nylon brackets reduce limb inertia, smoothing acceleration curves.  
- Joint offsets maintain foot clearance and keep Cg within safe bounds.  
- Step sequence phasing coordinated via Reactive Controller and watchdog timing (≤ 250 ms sync margin).  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. LOAD AND STABILITY CONSIDERATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

- Each supporting leg rated for ≤ 40 % dynamic load during transition.  
- Cg vector actively biased toward grounded tripod set to maintain balance.  
- Minimum 3 points of contact maintained throughout sequence.  
- Actuator torques restricted to 90 % of nominal continuous rating.  
- Printed limb mass reduction lowers peak torque by ≈ 12 %.  
- Safety thresholds monitored by Reactive Safety Controller (Tripod_Gait Profile).  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. ARMOR AND PAYLOAD CLEARANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

- Retraction angles calculated to avoid armor and torso collision using geometric envelope /01_Mechanical/Mechanical_Layout.txt.  
- Payload modules maintain center‑of‑mass alignment based on Neutral Stance Geometry.  
- Collision forecast performed via LIDAR depth map prior to foot lift (100 Hz check rate).  
- Abort conditions: detected interference < 30 mm clearance → Reactive STOP.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. CONTROL INTEGRATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Reactive Controller monitors:
- Step height and incline sensors  
- Ground reaction force sensors  
- Limb position and joint current feedback  

**Control Hierarchy:**  
1. Hardware Kill‑Line  
2. Reactive Safety Preemption  
3. Mission Compute (Stair‑Mode Sequence)  
4. Remote or Swarm Operator Commands  

Swarm inputs cannot override Reactive Stair‑Mode controllers.  
Watchdog enforces heartbeat timeout (≤ 500 ms) to ensure authority consistency.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. VALIDATION AND TESTING
------------------------------------------------------------

**Simulation Tests:**  
- Multi‑step ascent/descent (0.2 – 0.4 m step height range) with varying payloads and printed modules.  
- Torque and thermal profiles monitored per leg.  

**Field Tests:**  
- Real stair sets 0.2 m, 0.3 m, 0.4 m.  
- Data logged: torque utilization, Cg deviation, actuator temperature, slip rate.  

FMEA Cross‑References:  
- FM‑003 Battery Undervoltage  
- FM‑004 Servo Overheating  
- FM‑008 IMU Drift  

All validation records archived under /09_Testing/Stair_Mode_Validation/.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Provide repeatable and safe stair traversal with hardware‑dominant safety behavior.  
- Maintain structural integrity, actuator protection, and balance through full cycle.  
- Integrate tightly with Neutral Stance Geometry, Mass and Cg Model, and Control Authority Model.  
- Ensure operator and swarm commands respect Reactive Safety hierarchy at all times.  
- Support field‑replaceable modular printed limbs & brackets for service sustainability.  
- Extend verified tripod gait principles to multi‑step terrain operations.
