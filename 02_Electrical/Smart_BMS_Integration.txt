1. PURPOSE

Define integration of the Smart Battery Management System (BMS) within OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures safe battery operation and system-wide power awareness
Provides real-time telemetry for control and safety systems
Enables predictive maintenance and fault detection
Integrates with Main Power System, Energy Models, and Control Authority
2. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

The Smart BMS is responsible for:

Monitoring battery health and operating conditions
Protecting against unsafe electrical states
Providing telemetry to Mission Compute and Reactive Safety layers
Enforcing hardware-level safety shutdown when required

BMS operates as a critical safety subsystem with direct authority over power delivery.

3. MONITORED PARAMETERS
Parameter	Description	Source
Pack Voltage	Total battery voltage	BMS ADC
Cell Voltages	Individual cell monitoring	BMS ADC
Current	Charge/discharge current	Shunt sensor
Temperature	Cell and pack temperature	Thermistors
State of Charge (SOC)	Remaining capacity (%)	Calculated
State of Health (SOH)	Battery degradation estimate	Calculated
All parameters updated in real-time and logged for diagnostics.
4. PROTECTION FUNCTIONS
Overvoltage Protection
Undervoltage Protection
Overcurrent Protection
Short-Circuit Protection
Thermal Shutdown (High/Low temperature limits)
Cell Balancing (passive or active)

Protection actions include:

Limiting current draw
Isolating battery from system
Triggering system-wide safe shutdown
5. CONTROL INTERFACES
Mission Compute Interface:
Receives telemetry via UART / CAN / I2C (implementation dependent)
Uses SOC and current data for energy-aware motion planning
Reactive Safety Layer:
Receives critical alerts (undervoltage, thermal fault)
Initiates immediate safe-stop if thresholds exceeded
Hardware Interface:
Direct control of power cutoff (main bus disconnect)
Independent of software execution
6. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS
Supported interfaces:
CAN bus (preferred for robustness)
UART (fallback / simple integration)
Requirements:
Error detection (CRC or equivalent)
Message timeout handling
Redundant reporting of critical parameters
7. FAILSAFE AND AUTHORITY BEHAVIOR
BMS has hardware-level authority to:
Disconnect battery under fault conditions
Override Mission Compute commands
In failure scenarios:
Loss of communication → system assumes worst-case and reduces load
Critical fault → immediate power cutoff
Safety priority rule enforced:
BMS actions must not interfere with kill-line or watchdog operation
Power cutoff must result in predictable safe-stop behavior
8. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER SYSTEMS
Energy Models:
LiFePO4 and LiPo SOC/voltage curves derived from BMS data
Main Power System:
BMS sits between battery and main bus
Servo Bank Architecture:
BMS informs load balancing and current limiting
Deployment Profile:
Records BMS firmware version, configuration, and calibration
FMEA References:
FM-003 (undervoltage)
FM-009 (power fault)
9. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Functional testing of all protection triggers
SOC accuracy validation via full charge/discharge cycles
Communication reliability under load
Thermal shutdown verification
Fault injection testing (overcurrent, undervoltage, sensor failure)

All results documented per Configuration Management Plan.

10. DESIGN INTENT
Provide intelligent, autonomous battery protection
Enable real-time energy awareness across the system
Ensure safe and deterministic response to electrical faults
Support predictive maintenance and long-term reliability
Fully integrate with control authority, FMEA, and deployment systems
