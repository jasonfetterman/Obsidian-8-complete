1. PURPOSE

Define the energy storage model for OBSIDIAN-8 V3 using LiFePO4 batteries.

Supports safe operation, predictable performance, and integration with reactive control.
Provides reference for FMEA, deployment profiles, and CM documentation.
Ensures compatibility with all actuators, sensors, and power electronics.
2. BATTERY SPECIFICATIONS
Parameter	Value / Range	Notes
Chemistry	LiFePO4	High cycle life, safe chemistry
Nominal Voltage	12.8 V	4 cells in series
Capacity	20 Ah	Per module
Max Continuous Discharge	15 A	Limited to prevent overheating
Peak Discharge	30 A	Short duration < 10 s
Charge Voltage	14.6 V	Constant voltage charge
Charge Current	5 A	Recommended per module
Depth of Discharge (DoD)	80%	Maintain long life
Cycle Life	>2000 cycles	At 80% DoD
3. ENERGY MANAGEMENT MODEL
State-of-Charge (SOC) Calculation:
SOC (%) = (Remaining Capacity / Total Capacity) × 100
Updated in real-time via BMS telemetry.
Voltage Modeling:
Open-circuit voltage (OCV) curve mapped vs SOC.
Load-dependent voltage drop considered for actuator torque calculation.
Thermal Considerations:
Temperature sensors integrated.
Derate output current above 50°C to prevent damage.
Overcurrent / Undervoltage Protection:
BMS limits draw to max continuous discharge.
System triggers safe shutdown at undervoltage (<10.5 V per module).
4. DEPLOYMENT INTEGRATION
Battery module identifiers stored in deployment_profile.
SOC limits integrated with Energy Model FMEA:
FM-003: Undervoltage during movement → triggers pre-cutoff stop.
Batteries monitored via telemetry for predictive maintenance.
5. CONTROL INTERFACES
Actuator Power Management:
Limits maximum torque based on available energy.
Prevents simultaneous high-current draw across all actuators exceeding BMS limits.
Reactive Safety Layer:
Overrides mission compute if SOC drops below minimum safe threshold (20%).
Ensures predictable behavior under low-energy conditions.
Charging / Swapping:
Supports hot-swap of modules with automatic SOC synchronization.
Charging follows manufacturer-recommended CC-CV protocol.
6. VALIDATION AND TESTING
SOC calibration verified with full discharge tests.
Voltage response under nominal and peak load simulated.
Thermal performance tested in environmental chamber (-20°C to 60°C).
Integration tested with Mass_and_CG_Model, Structural_Load_Model, and Actuation Systems for energy-aware motion.
7. DESIGN INTENT
Maintain predictable power availability under all operational scenarios.
Ensure safety of electronics and actuators.
Fully compatible with CM, FMEA, deployment profiles, and control authority.
Support reactive and mission-level decision-making with accurate SOC and energy forecasting.
