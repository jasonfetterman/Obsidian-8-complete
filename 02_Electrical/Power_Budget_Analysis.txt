Power Budget Analysis — REV C (16-Servo Octopod, High-Torque System)

OVERVIEW
This document defines the electrical power requirements for OBSIDIAN-8 configured with 16 high-torque servos (150KG-class), onboard compute, and supporting electronics.

--------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM VOLTAGES

Servo Power Rail:
- Nominal: 7.4V (2S LiPo)
- Range: 6.4V – 8.4V

Logic Power Rail:
- 5V regulated (buck converter from main battery)

--------------------------------------------------

SERVO POWER CHARACTERISTICS

Servo Class:
- 150KG·cm high-torque digital servos

Per-Servo Estimates (based on typical spec for this class):
- Idle: 0.2 A
- Moving (no load): 1.5 A
- Moderate load: 4–6 A
- Peak stall: 8–10 A

Design Basis (used for calculations):
- Average active current per servo: 5 A
- Peak transient per servo: 8 A

--------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM CURRENT CALCULATION

Total Servos:
- 16

Average Operating Current:
- 16 × 5 A = 80 A

Peak Transient Current:
- 16 × 8 A = 128 A

Realistic Peak (wave gait, not all stall simultaneously):
- 60–90 A typical spikes
- Worst-case electrical design must handle 120 A

--------------------------------------------------

LOGIC SYSTEM LOAD

Raspberry Pi:
- 2.5 – 3.0 A @ 5V

Teensy:
- ~0.1 A

Sensors + peripherals:
- ~0.5 A

Total Logic Load:
- ~3.5 – 4.0 A @ 5V

Converted to battery side (~7.4V with 90% efficiency):
- ~2.5 – 3.0 A

--------------------------------------------------

TOTAL SYSTEM LOAD

Average Total Current:
- ~82–85 A

Peak Total Current:
- ~120–130 A

--------------------------------------------------

BATTERY REQUIREMENTS

Battery Type:
- LiPo (high discharge)

Recommended Configuration:
- 2S (7.4V nominal)

Capacity:
- Minimum: 5000 mAh
- Recommended: 8000–10000 mAh

Discharge Rating:

Required Continuous Current:
- ≥ 100 A

Required Burst Current:
- ≥ 130 A

C Rating Calculation (example 8000 mAh):
- 8 Ah × 20C = 160 A (acceptable)
- Recommended minimum: 25C

FINAL SPEC:
- 2S 8000 mAh 25C (or higher)

--------------------------------------------------

POWER DISTRIBUTION

Main Battery → XT90 Connector → Main Fuse → Power Bus

Branches:
- Servo Power Bus (direct high-current path)
- Buck Converter → 5V Logic Rail

--------------------------------------------------

PROTECTION

Main Fuse:
- 120 A automotive fuse (slow-blow)

Per-Branch Protection:
- Optional 20–30 A branch fuses for servo groups

Kill Switch:
- Inline with servo power rail only

--------------------------------------------------

WIRING REQUIREMENTS

Main Battery Leads:
- 10–12 AWG

Servo Distribution:
- 14–16 AWG trunk lines
- 18 AWG to individual servos

Grounding:
- Common ground reference required

--------------------------------------------------

CAPACITANCE (CRITICAL)

Bulk Capacitors on Servo Rail:
- Minimum: 2 × 2200 µF low-ESR electrolytic

Placement:
- Near servo distribution center

Purpose:
- Absorb transient current spikes
- Reduce voltage sag

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL LOAD

High Current Areas:
- Battery leads
- Connectors
- Distribution points

Requirements:
- Airflow around power components
- No enclosed heat buildup

--------------------------------------------------

RUNTIME ESTIMATION

Example: 8000 mAh battery

Average draw: 80 A

Runtime:
- 8 Ah / 80 A = 0.1 hr ≈ 6 minutes

Realistic mixed usage:
- 6–10 minutes depending on gait and load

--------------------------------------------------

FAILURE RISKS

Undersized battery:
- Voltage sag
- System resets

Undersized wiring:
- Overheating
- Fire risk

Insufficient capacitance:
- Control instability

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

- System must handle 120 A peak without failure
- Voltage must remain stable under load
- Wiring must be rated above maximum current
- Logic rail must remain isolated and stable
