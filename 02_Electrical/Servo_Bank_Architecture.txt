1. PURPOSE

Define the architecture, grouping, power distribution, and control interfaces for servo actuator banks in OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures stable and scalable actuator control
Prevents power overload and signal interference
Integrates with Main Power System, Control Authority Model, and FMEA
Supports modular limb configurations (e.g., Leg_MK1, future variants)
2. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Servo actuators are organized into independent banks, each responsible for a subset of limbs or joints.

Each servo bank includes:

Dedicated power distribution line
Signal/control interface (PWM / serial bus)
Local protection (fuse or current limiter)
Ground reference tied to system ground
3. SERVO BANK GROUPING
Bank ID	Assigned Subsystem	Typical Load	Notes
SB-01	Front Left Leg (FL)	High	Hip, knee, ankle
SB-02	Front Right Leg (FR)	High	
SB-03	Rear Left Leg (RL)	High	
SB-04	Rear Right Leg (RR)	High	
Each bank operates independently to prevent cascading failures.
Additional banks may be defined for arms or payload actuators.
4. POWER DISTRIBUTION
Servo banks powered from Actuator Bus (12V nominal).
Each bank includes:
Inline fuse or resettable breaker
Local bulk capacitance for transient load smoothing
Design constraints:
Max current per bank limited per actuator specifications
Simultaneous peak loads across banks controlled by Mission Compute
5. SIGNAL AND CONTROL INTERFACE
Control methods:
PWM (standard servo control) or
Serial bus (preferred for scalability and noise resistance)
Requirements:
Shielded signal lines in high EMI environments
Common ground reference with power system
Update rate sufficient for real-time motion control
ROS2 interface connects Mission Compute to servo controllers.
6. LOAD MANAGEMENT AND PROTECTION
Current Limiting:
Prevents overload during high torque demand
Thermal Monitoring:
Servo temperature monitored where supported
Dynamic Load Balancing:
Mission Compute staggers motion to avoid simultaneous peak draw
Fault Isolation:
Failure in one bank must not affect others
7. CONTROL AUTHORITY INTEGRATION
Hardware Level:
Kill-line disables power to all servo banks
Reactive Controller:
Overrides servo commands for collision avoidance or instability
Mission Compute:
Issues coordinated movement commands across banks
Failsafe Behavior:
On communication loss or watchdog trigger → all banks enter safe-stop state
8. CABLE ROUTING AND PHYSICAL INTEGRATION
Power and signal cables routed per Cable_Routing_and_Strain_Control.txt
Strain relief applied at:
Joint entry points
Servo connectors
Banks physically distributed to:
Minimize cable length
Reduce voltage drop
Improve modularity
9. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Load testing per bank under maximum torque conditions
Simulated multi-bank peak load scenarios
Signal integrity testing under full system operation
FMEA references:
FM-004 (servo overheating)
FM-009 (power distribution fault)
10. DESIGN INTENT
Provide scalable and fault-tolerant actuator control architecture
Ensure stable power delivery and signal integrity
Prevent cascading failures across actuator systems
Enable precise, coordinated motion across all limbs
Fully integrate with CM, FMEA, deployment profiles, and control authority hierarchy
