Main Power System — REV B (150KG Servo Integration)

OVERVIEW
This document defines the primary power system architecture for OBSIDIAN-8. The system is designed to support high-current 150KG-class servos while maintaining stable, isolated power for control electronics.

--------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The power system is divided into two isolated domains:

1. High-Current Servo Power System
2. Regulated Logic Power System

Both systems share a common ground but are electrically isolated in power delivery.

--------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY POWER SOURCE

Battery Type:
- Lithium Polymer (LiPo)

Configuration:
- 2S (7.4V nominal, 8.4V fully charged)

Capacity:
- Minimum: 5000mAh
- Recommended: 6000mAh–10000mAh for extended runtime

Discharge Rating:
- Minimum: 50C

Connector:
- XT60 (minimum)
- XT90 (recommended for higher reliability under load)

--------------------------------------------------

POWER FLOW

Battery Output
   → Master Power Switch
   → Main Fuse (60A–80A)
   → Power Distribution Block

From Distribution Block:
   → Servo Power Rail (7.4V direct)
   → Buck Converter → Logic Power Rail (5V regulated)

--------------------------------------------------

SERVO POWER SYSTEM

Voltage:
- Direct battery voltage (7.4V nominal)

Load Characteristics:
- High current spikes (up to 80A peak theoretical)
- Rapid transient demand

Requirements:
- Low resistance wiring
- Short power paths
- High-current rated connectors

Distribution:
- Star topology from central power block
- No daisy-chaining between servos

--------------------------------------------------

LOGIC POWER SYSTEM

Source:
- Buck converter from main battery

Output:
- 5V regulated

Capacity:
- Minimum: 5A continuous

Supplies:
- Raspberry Pi or main controller
- Sensors
- Communication modules

Critical Rule:
- Logic power must remain stable regardless of servo load

--------------------------------------------------

GROUNDING STRATEGY

- Common ground between all systems is REQUIRED
- Use a central grounding point at the power distribution block
- Avoid ground loops by preventing multiple return paths

--------------------------------------------------

PROTECTION SYSTEMS

Required:
- Main fuse (60A–80A) at battery output
- Inline fuses for each servo branch (15A typical)
- Properly rated wiring and connectors

Recommended:
- Emergency kill switch (cuts battery output)
- Electronic watchdog kill line (integrated with control system)

--------------------------------------------------

VOLTAGE STABILITY

To prevent system instability:

- Install bulk capacitors (2200µF–4700µF) on each servo branch
- Use low-ESR capacitors
- Keep all high-current paths as short as possible

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL MANAGEMENT

Heat sources:
- Battery under high discharge
- Wiring and connectors
- Voltage regulators

Mitigation:
- Provide airflow around battery and power components
- Avoid enclosed, unventilated compartments
- Use appropriately rated components to reduce resistive heating

--------------------------------------------------

FAILURE MODES

Potential Failures:
- Voltage sag under load
- Controller reset due to unstable logic rail
- Connector overheating or failure
- Fuse trip under sustained high load

High-Risk Conditions:
- Simultaneous multi-servo movement
- Rapid direction changes
- Low battery voltage

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

- Never power servos from logic regulators
- Never share logic and servo power rails directly
- Always design for peak current, not average current
- Maintain at least 30% overhead in power capacity
