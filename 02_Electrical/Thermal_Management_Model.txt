1. PURPOSE

Define thermal behavior, heat sources, dissipation methods, and protection mechanisms for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures safe operating temperatures across all subsystems
Prevents thermal-induced failures and degradation
Integrates with power system, actuation, and control authority
Supports validation, FMEA, and deployment readiness
2. HEAT SOURCES
Component	Heat Source Description	Relative Load
Servo Actuators	Motor current and friction	High
DC-DC Regulators	Conversion losses	Medium
Battery Modules	Internal resistance during load	Medium
BMS	Monitoring circuitry	Low
Mission Compute Unit	CPU/GPU processing	Medium
Peak thermal load occurs during simultaneous actuator motion + high compute usage.
3. TEMPERATURE LIMITS
Component	Max Operating Temp	Critical Shutdown
Servo Actuators	70°C	80°C
LiFePO4 Battery	60°C	70°C
LiPo Battery	60°C	65°C
DC-DC Regulators	80°C	90°C
Compute Unit	75°C	85°C
Exceeding critical thresholds triggers reactive safety shutdown.
4. THERMAL DISTRIBUTION MODEL
Heat concentrated in:
Actuator joints
Power distribution zones
Compute enclosure
Frame acts as passive heat sink, distributing heat across structure.
Thermal gradients minimized by:
Centralized battery placement
Distributed actuator loads
Ventilated regulator mounting
5. COOLING STRATEGIES

Passive Cooling:

Aluminum chassis for heat conduction
Ventilated panels for airflow
Heat sinks on regulators and compute modules

Active Cooling (if installed):

Low-profile fans in compute and power zones
Temperature-triggered activation

Thermal Isolation:

Separation of battery and high-heat components
Insulating mounts where required
6. SENSOR AND MONITORING INTEGRATION
Temperature sensors located at:
Battery modules
Servo banks (if supported)
Power distribution board
Compute unit
Data integrated into:
Mission Compute telemetry
Reactive Safety Layer
Sampling rate sufficient for real-time protection decisions
7. CONTROL INTEGRATION
Mission Compute:
Adjusts workload and actuator usage based on thermal conditions
Reduces motion intensity under elevated temperatures
Reactive Safety Layer:
Initiates safe-stop when thermal limits exceeded
Overrides mission commands if necessary
BMS Integration:
Enforces battery thermal limits and shutdown
System Control Authority:
Hardware shutdown takes precedence over all software control
8. FAILURE MODES AND MITIGATION
Failure Mode	Cause	Mitigation
Servo overheating	High torque load	Torque limiting, duty cycling
Battery overheating	Overcurrent / ambient	BMS shutdown, load reduction
Regulator overheating	Inefficiency / load	Heat sinks, airflow
Compute thermal throttling	High processing load	Active cooling, load balancing
FMEA references: FM-004 (servo overheating), FM-003 (battery undervoltage via thermal stress).
9. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Thermal profiling under:
Idle conditions
Continuous motion
Peak load scenarios
Environmental testing:
-20°C to 60°C ambient range
Verification includes:
Sensor accuracy
Cooling effectiveness
Shutdown response timing
All results documented per Configuration Management Plan
10. DESIGN INTENT
Maintain safe operating temperatures across all subsystems
Prevent thermal-induced failures and performance degradation
Enable predictive and reactive thermal control
Integrate fully with power, actuation, and safety systems
Ensure deterministic and safe response to overheating conditions
