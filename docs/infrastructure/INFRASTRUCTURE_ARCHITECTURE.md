\# INFRASTRUCTURE ARCHITECTURE — OBSIDIAN-8



\## Role

The Infrastructure Layer provides all sustaining systems required for continuous operation of the OBSIDIAN-8 ecosystem, including energy, fabrication, maintenance, and deployment support.



\---



\## Core Responsibilities



\- Energy distribution and power management

\- Automated charging and docking systems

\- Fabrication and rapid part production

\- Maintenance scheduling and diagnostics

\- Fleet deployment support and staging



\---



\## Subsystems



\### 1. Charging Systems

Manages energy replenishment for all fleet units, including wired, wireless, and docking-based charging.



\### 2. Docking Systems

Handles physical integration points for fleet ingress, egress, and maintenance staging.



\### 3. Fabrication Systems

Supports additive manufacturing (3D printing), repair part production, and structural component fabrication.



\### 4. Maintenance Systems

Monitors fleet wear, failure prediction, and automated repair workflows.



\---



\## Operational Model



\- Infrastructure systems operate continuously in background mode

\- Fleet units interface via standardized docking protocols

\- Command Layer can prioritize infrastructure allocation during emergencies

\- Swarm Layer may request infrastructure services dynamically



\---



\## Design Principles



\- Always-available support layer

\- Redundant fail-safe systems

\- Minimal manual intervention required

\- Standardized interfaces across all subsystems
